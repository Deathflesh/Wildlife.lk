WHERE TO VISIT

Explore Sri Lanka's diverse and captivating wildlife beyond the renowned Yala and Wilpattu. Venture into lesser-explored sanctuaries like Sinharaja Rainforest, home to a rich tapestry of flora and fauna. Discover the lush landscapes of Udawalawe National Park, a haven for elephants and myriad bird species. Traverse the scenic Horton Plains, where elusive leopards and endemic species thrive in a stunning natural setting. Sri Lanka's hidden wildlife gems await your discovery, promising unforgettable encounters with nature's wonders.

Sinharaja Rainforest

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, brimming with diverse species of birds, amphibians, and rare trees, offering a glimpse into Sri Lanka's tropical biodiversity.

Udawalawe National Park

Known for its large elephant population, this park also hosts a variety of mammals, birds, and reptiles amidst its scenic grasslands and reservoirs.

Knuckles Mountain Range

Rich in biodiversity and scenic beauty, this range is home to numerous endemic species and provides fantastic trekking opportunities.
